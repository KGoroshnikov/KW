EXOSPHERE

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. Its orbital period around the Sun of 88 days is the shortest of all the planets in the Solar System.

Like Venus, Mercury orbits the Sun within Earth's orbit as an inferior planet, so it can only be seen visually in the morning or the evening sky, and never exceed 28deg away from the Sun.

Containing hydrogen, hellium, oxygen, sodium, calcium.

CLIMATE

Mercury's climate is characterized by extreme temperature variations due to its lack of a significant atmosphere to retain heat. Daytime temperatures on the sunlit side can soar to around 430 degrees Celsius, while nighttime temperatures on the dark side plummet to approximately -180 degrees Celsius.

This stark temperature dichotomy results from the planet's slow rotation and thin atmosphere, unable to regulate and distribute heat effectively.

PHYSICAL

Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System, with a rocky surface marked by vast plains, rugged highlands, and numerous impact craters. Its thin atmosphere contributes to extreme temperature variations, ranging from scorching days to freezing nights.

Mercury's dense iron-nickel core generates a weak magnetic field. The planet's slow rotation takes about 59 Earth days, and it completes an orbit around the Sun in just 88 Earth days.
